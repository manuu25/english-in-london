From Brazil • 1 Year Journey

Maria's Journey: From Scared to Confident

"I arrived in London in January 2024 with basic English and zero friends. I was so scared to speak that I ordered food by pointing at pictures for my first two weeks. Everything changed when I found EnglishInLondon."

"Within two weeks of joining the conversation circle, I had my first real English conversation over coffee. Six months later, I'm helping other new Brazilian students find their confidence too."
15+

Maria's tip: "Don't wait to feel ready. Join a conversation group on your first week. The community will catch you when you stumble, and celebrate when you soar."

From Egypt • 10 Month Journey

Ahmed's Journey: Finding His London Family

"I came to London for my Master's degree, but I was struggling with academic English and feeling isolated. The buddy program didn't just improve my English – it gave me a brother."

"My buddy James showed me his London – the hidden pubs, the weekend markets, the football culture. Now it's my London too. We still meet every week, even though my English is fluent now."

Ahmed's London Discovery List:

  • • Borough Market Saturday mornings
  • • Arsenal matches at the local pub
  • • Sunday roasts with James's family
  • • Night photography walks along the Thames

Ahmed's advice: "A buddy isn't just for English practice. They're your bridge to understanding British culture and feeling like you belong here."

From Japan • 8 Month Journey

Yuki's Journey: From Silent to Speaking Up

"In Japan, I was good at English grammar and reading, but speaking terrified me. I thought I just needed to memorize more words. The community taught me that English is about connection, not perfection."

Before

  • • Avoided group conversations
  • • Studied alone for hours
  • • Afraid of making mistakes

After

  • • Leads conversation groups
  • • Learns through community events
  • • Celebrates progress over perfection
"I discovered that my accent isn't something to hide – it's part of my story. Now I help other Japanese students embrace their voices too."

Yuki's wisdom: "Perfect English doesn't exist. Confident English does, and it comes from practice, patience, and people who believe in you."
